Figma is a browser-based design tool for creating interfaces, graphics, and prototypes. Before diving into advanced features, you need to be fluent in the workspace and core tools.

## Navigate the workspace

Learn the canvas, toolbar, layers panel, design panel, pages, and navigation shortcuts.

<YouTube id="zt-Zr57Vfzs" />

- [Figma Design for Beginners](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/sections/30880632542743) — Official 33-chapter course (~3 hours). Start here.
- [Explore the canvas](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/360041064814) — Pan, zoom, and navigate.

<Skill id="figma-navigate-interface" label="Navigate the Figma workspace, panels, and canvas" />

## Frames and groups

Frames are Figma's primary container. They clip content, support layout, and can be nested. Groups are for quick temporary selections.

<YouTube id="II-6dDzc-80" />

- [Frames and groups](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/360041539473) — The difference and when to use each.

<Skill id="figma-frames-groups" label="Create and nest frames, and understand frames vs groups" />

## Shapes and text

Draw rectangles, ellipses, polygons, and lines. Create and style text layers with font, size, line height, and alignment controls.

- [Shape tools](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/360040450133) — Draw and style basic shapes.
- [Text](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/360039956634) — Typography controls and text resizing.

<Skill id="figma-shapes-text" label="Draw shapes and create styled text layers" />

## Images, masks, and booleans

Import images as fills, create masks to clip content, and combine shapes with boolean operations (union, subtract, intersect, exclude).

- [Images](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/360040028034) — Add, crop, and adjust images.
- [Boolean operations](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/360039957534) — Combine shapes into complex forms.

<Skill id="figma-images-booleans" label="Work with images, masks, and boolean operations" />
