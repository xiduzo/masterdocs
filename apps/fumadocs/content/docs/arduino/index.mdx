This learning path takes you from your first blinking LED to building connected hardware that talks to IoT platforms and creative tools. Each topic gives you a brief overview then points you to the best official resources.

## What you'll learn

### Arduino Fundamentals

Set up the IDE, write your first sketch, understand digital and analog I/O, learn breadboard prototyping and circuit safety, and master essential programming patterns like serial communication and non-blocking timing.

### Hardware Components

Work with sensors (temperature, ultrasonic, motion, light), displays (LED, LCD, OLED), and actuators (servos, DC motors, stepper motors, relays).

### Connectivity and IoT

Connect your Arduino to WiFi with ESP32/ESP8266, communicate over MQTT, and integrate with IoT platforms and brokers.

### Creative Integrations

Bridge Arduino to creative tools like TouchDesigner (serial/OSC), Microflow (visual flow programming), Node-RED, Processing, and p5.js.
