Actuators let your Arduino interact with the physical world — rotate, push, switch, and move.

## Servo motors

Position a servo precisely by angle (0–180°) using the built-in Servo library. Great for robotic arms, pan/tilt cameras, and locks.

- [Servo Motors](https://docs.arduino.cc/learn/electronics/servo-motors/) — How servos work and how to control them.

<Skill id="arduino-servo" label="Control servo motor position with the Servo library" />

## DC motors

Drive DC motors for continuous rotation using transistors or motor driver shields. Control speed with PWM and direction with an H-bridge.

- [Transistor Motor Control](https://docs.arduino.cc/learn/electronics/transistor-motor-control/) — Drive a DC motor with a transistor and PWM.
- [Motor Shield Rev3](https://docs.arduino.cc/tutorials/motor-shield-rev3/msr3-controlling-dc-motor/) — Using the official Arduino Motor Shield.

<Skill id="arduino-dc-motor" label="Drive DC motors with transistors or motor shields" />

## Stepper motors

Move in precise steps for CNC machines, 3D printers, and robotics. Use the Stepper or AccelStepper library.

- [Stepper Motors](https://docs.arduino.cc/learn/electronics/stepper-motors/) — Stepper motor configurations and wiring.

<Skill id="arduino-stepper" label="Control stepper motors for precise rotational movement" />

## Relays

Switch high-voltage devices (lamps, fans, appliances) on and off safely from a low-voltage Arduino pin.

- [4 Relays Shield Basics](https://docs.arduino.cc/tutorials/4-relays-shield/4-relay-shield-basics/) — Control relays with the Arduino Relay Shield.

<Skill id="arduino-relay" label="Switch high-voltage devices safely using relay modules" />
