Before wiring up components, you need to understand how a breadboard works, how power flows through your circuit, and how to avoid frying your board.

## Breadboard and wiring basics

Understand breadboard layout (power rails, rows, the center gap), how to place components, and how to wire connections cleanly.

- [Arduino to Breadboard](https://docs.arduino.cc/built-in-examples/arduino-isp/ArduinoToBreadboard/) — Running an Arduino on a breadboard.
- [Built-in Examples](https://docs.arduino.cc/built-in-examples/) — All official examples include wiring diagrams you can follow.

## Power management and voltage levels

Know the difference between 5V and 3.3V logic, how much current a pin can source/sink, and how to safely power external components.

- [5V and 3.3V Logic Levels](https://docs.arduino.cc/learn/microcontrollers/5v-3v3/) — Voltage level differences and level shifting.
- [Power Tree](https://docs.arduino.cc/learn/electronics/power-tree/) — How power flows through an Arduino board.
- [Low Power Guide](https://docs.arduino.cc/learn/electronics/low-power/) — Reducing power consumption for battery projects.
